About MacDonalds

McDonald's is an American fast food chain that began in San Bernardino, California, in 1940 as a restaurant run by Richard and Maurice McDonald. They renamed their business a hamburger stand and then transformed it into a franchise, with the Golden Arches emblem first appearing in 1953 at a Phoenix, Arizona site. In the year 1955. McDonald's is the world's most profitable restaurant business, with over 69 million customers served daily in over 100 countries and 37,855 locations as of 2018. McDonald's is most known for their hamburgers, cheeseburgers, and french fries, but they also provide chicken, breakfast, soft drinks, milkshakes, wraps, and desserts.
